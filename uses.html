The Olive Fruit

The olive is a drupe fruit with oily flesh and a large pit.

Olive Oil

Olive oil is a fat obtained from the fruit of the olive tree. The first pressing of the fruit is labeled as extra virgin olive oil or EVOO. Olive oil is edible, but has also been used as a fuel (lamps) and for religious purposes.

Olive Wood

Olive wood is a very dense hardwood that is prized not just for its durability, but for its beautiful grain pattern as well. It is often used for kitchen utensils due to its ability to withstand high heat.

Pomace

The leftovers from the oil extraction forms a mush that is called pomace. Further extraction can be performed with the help of heat and solvents. This is sometimes consumed and also has industrial applications.

The olive plant has so many uses. It is no wonder that this tree has been an essential part of many people's lives for thousands of years.
