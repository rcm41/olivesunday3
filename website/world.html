Olives are currently cultivated in many parts of the world.

Because olives are such delicious and useful fruit, olive trees and seeds have been carried from the Mediterranean region to many parts of the globe for thousands of years.

Spain, Italy, and Greece are the world's largest producers of olives. In the United States, California is the major producer of olives. Other states like Florida, Georgia, and Arizona are beginning olive cultivation, but it will take them a long time to catch up to California's production levels.

Next time you are traveling...be sure to try the local olives!
